Making Personal Websites

That reflect your aesthetic, say something about who you are, and which document your experience and projects

Product Goals

For each young person participating in the eighth grade hackathon to create a personal website that:

  • Works on computers and phones
  • Is something they’re proud to show their friends and families
  • Which captures their experience in the 8th grade hackathon
  • Which incorporates their personal aesthetic

Process Goals

Each young person leaves this experience being able to:

  • Understand the relationship between an HTML document and the browser
  • Can translate something they care about into a website.

General Arc/Flow

  1. Each student will download sublime text, git, node.js, and giphy capture from the Dalton Software Center.
  2. While those are downloading, we’ll look at some great examples of personal websites and talk about what we think makes them great.
  3. They’ll look at examples of and talk about about what great documentation looks like.
  4. They’ll inspect some websites they love and some personal websites we give them in the console. MJ will live code in front of them about how to make and style in HTML and CSS:
    • A div
    • An anchor link
    • An image and related cool filters
  5. They’ll be given several “templates” (HTML Files) of personal websites to choose from, which they can modify to whatever degree they want so that they don’t even look like their original form at all. Each template will primarily be a journal that they’ll be styling and adding content to throughout their time in intersession. This reflective process will be what they do with their house advisors at the end of the day.

Templates

Each student will choose the template they want and modify it to make their own website — HS The templates on our menu:

  • “A website’s website”: A template with a menu bar with links for each [Day, breakthrough moment, experience, etc.] (e.g. Ella, Ciara)
  • “For the image-inary…”:The image/div for each [Day, breakthrough moment, experience, etc.] (e.g. Molly, Roly, Dagmar, Spencer)
  • “Image-in”: images and photographs representing each experience (e.g. Spencer's website)
  • “The click down scroll down”: Sections separating experience (e.g. This Teenage Life, PHS, CS1)
  • “The elegant scroll-down”: Plain text of text with images capturing things continuously. Could incorporate some cool “parallax” effects. (e.g. Hungry month of March, Katie, Deryk Georke, This Teenage Life/CS1)
